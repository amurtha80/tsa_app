On TSA in your branding

The TSA insignia is a registered trademark — but that covers their logo and visual identity, not the acronym itself as a descriptive term. The key legal distinction is trademark use vs. descriptive use. You're allowed to say "TSA wait times" in your app copy, descriptions, and social posts because you're describing what the thing is — that's fair descriptive use, not trademark infringement. What you can't do is use their logo, imply government affiliation, or name your product something like "OfficialTSA.app." 
Transportation Security Administration

Practically speaking: every competitor you'd find in a search — MyTSA trackers, travel blogs, third-party apps — freely uses "TSA" as a descriptive term. Nobody is getting cease-and-desisted for saying "TSA wait times."

What you cannot do safely:

Use TSA's eagle insignia/logo anywhere
Name the product itself "TSA [anything]" as a brand name
Imply your app is official, government-affiliated, or endorsed by TSA
What you can do freely:

"Track TSA wait times at major US airports" in your description
"TSA security checkpoint data" in your metadata/SEO copy
Reference TSA PreCheck by name (it's a program name, widely used descriptively)
